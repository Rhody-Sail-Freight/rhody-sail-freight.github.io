Farm to Table, By Sail

A vision for zero-emission cargo delivery across Narragansett Bay — connecting local farms with restaurants through a network of volunteer sailors.

Get Involved

The Idea

Recreational sailors are already out on Narragansett Bay every week. Local farms near the shoreline grow produce that restaurants across the Bay want to serve. What if those sailors could carry that harvest on their trips — delivering fresh, local food by wind power alone?

That's the idea behind Rhody Sail Freight: a volunteer network that connects farmers, sailors, and restaurants into a simple, wind-powered supply chain. No engines, no emissions — just sails, good food, and community.

We're in the early stages of developing this concept. Rhode Island's compact coastline, active sailing community, and thriving local food scene make it an ideal place to try something like this.

How It Could Work

Imagine a cycle connecting land and sea, farm and fork — powered entirely by wind.

Who Could Be Involved

Whether you sail, grow, or serve — there could be a place for you.

Sailors

Sailors who already enjoy time on the Bay could carry local goods on their trips — turning a weekend sail into something meaningful.

Farmers

Farms near the water could reach new markets carbon-free — loading crates at a nearby dock instead of putting produce on a truck.

Restaurants

Restaurants could source hyper-local ingredients with a great story — fresh produce that arrived by sailboat the same day.

Narragansett Bay

We're imagining routes across Narragansett Bay — from Wickford to Bristol, Providence to Newport, and all the harbors in between.

Get Involved

We're looking for sailors, farmers, restaurant owners, and anyone excited about local food and sustainable transport to help shape this idea.

Our goal is to launch an initial pilot project in spring/summer 2026.
